SafetyCulture (formerly iAuditor) is one of the most widely-used inspection platforms globally. It's polished, well-supported, and the mobile experience is genuinely good. If you're running safety inspections across multiple locations, it does that job well.

But for UK construction specifically, there are three recurring reasons firms end up searching for a **SafetyCulture alternative**. The pricing model, the absence of UK-specific compliance workflows, and the fact that it was designed for inspections rather than for the full range of construction safety work (permits, RAMS, toolbox talks, inductions, incident reporting).

If you're evaluating alternatives, this piece walks through what SafetyCulture does well, where it struggles for UK sites, and five platforms worth putting on the shortlist.

## Why UK construction firms look for SafetyCulture alternatives

Three reasons come up repeatedly in conversations with UK site managers and H&S leads.

**Per-user pricing gets expensive fast.** SafetyCulture's Premium plan starts around $24 per user per month. On a UK construction project with 50 operatives, that's roughly £780 per month, or £9,400 per year, just for workforce seats. On larger sites with 150+ workers, it climbs to £22,000+ annually. For firms where safety software wasn't in last year's budget, this is a serious ask.

**UK construction compliance isn't native.** SafetyCulture is Australian-built and globally marketed. It handles inspections across industries. It does not come with pre-built UK permit types (hot work, confined space, height, electrical, excavation, lifting), CDM 2015 workflows, HSE-specific RAMS structures, or UK-aligned COSHH and LOLER registers. Teams have to build these from scratch using the platform's drag-and-drop template builder.

**It's an inspection tool, not a full construction safety platform.** SafetyCulture's core strength is checklists and audits. Permit lifecycles (issue, approve, active, pause, close-out with fire watch), multi-day site inductions with competency checks, toolbox talks with attendance tracking, incident reporting aligned to RIDDOR categories, equipment calibration and vehicle fleet management, these either don't exist or require heavy customisation on SafetyCulture.

For a facilities management firm running routine inspections, SafetyCulture is a strong fit. For a UK principal contractor running a live construction site, it often ends up being one of three or four tools used together, which defeats the point of digitising in the first place.

## What SafetyCulture does well (and where it falls short for UK sites)

Fair assessment, because SafetyCulture isn't a bad product. It just isn't always the right one for UK construction.

**Strengths:**
- Polished mobile app with offline capability
- Huge template library across industries
- Easy drag-and-drop template builder
- Good reporting dashboards for multi-site operations
- Strong global support (24/7 chat, documentation)
- Integrations with Procore, Microsoft Teams, Google Drive, others
- Real-time sync across devices
- Established user base (50M+ inspections globally)

**Weaknesses for UK construction specifically:**
- Per-user pricing scales badly with worker-heavy sites
- No native UK permit-to-work templates (hot work, confined space, etc. with proper lifecycle)
- CDM 2015 workflows absent
- RAMS structures are generic, not HSE-aligned
- Permit fire watch, close-out and expiry logic needs manual configuration
- RIDDOR categories not pre-built
- LOLER, PUWER, COSHH registers need manual setup
- UK-specific compliance language missing (subbie, operative, CSCS, SSSTS)
- Some users report sync issues in low-signal areas despite "offline mode"

Reviews on Capterra and Software Advice mention both the offline capability (praised) and occasional data loss when reconnecting (criticised). That's an ongoing issue for UK construction teams working in basements, tunnels and rural civils.

## How to choose the right fit for your sites

A framework that cuts through the marketing:

**1. Count your workforce and model the licence cost honestly.**
Take your average number of operatives on site per month. Multiply by per-user pricing. Does it fit the safety software budget? If worker seats alone push the cost over your ceiling, per-user platforms are out.

**2. List the UK permit types and compliance workflows you actually need.**
Hot work? Confined space? Height? Electrical? CDM Construction Phase Plans? RIDDOR-aligned incident categories? If the platform doesn't support these out of the box, you're paying to rebuild templates the alternative already has.

**3. Test offline mode in a real UK site environment.**
Don't trust the sales demo. Put the app on a phone, go to a basement or steel-framed building where signal drops, and complete a permit. Does it save? Does it sync when you come out? Does the timestamp stay true to when the action happened?

**4. Check worker adoption requirements.**
If your project has 50+ operatives, shared supervisor accounts will break the audit trail. Platforms where workers can have their own free accounts reduce this risk. [Free worker accounts](https://mysafeops.com/blog/free-safety-app-construction-workers) are the biggest adoption lever most sites underestimate.

**5. Get a trial with at least one real site.**
Sales demos don't tell you what a Tuesday at 7am looks like. Every serious platform offers a free trial. Use it on a live project for two weeks before signing anything.

## Questions to ask on any demo

When evaluating SafetyCulture or any alternative, ask these five questions directly. Genuine platforms answer them cleanly, hedge answers should raise flags.

**1. "What's the total annual cost for 50 workers, 5 supervisors, 2 admins?"**
Don't accept "it depends". Per-user platforms will be honest about the scaling cost. Platforms with different pricing models will show you the comparison.

**2. "Show me a confined space permit being issued, signed by the entrant, and closed with atmospheric test log, all offline."**
This single test exposes whether UK permit workflows are native or require custom templates.

**3. "Where's the RIDDOR reporting structure? Can I filter incidents by RIDDOR category?"**
If the answer is "we have a custom forms module, you could build it", the platform wasn't built for UK construction.

**4. "How does a new worker arriving on site Monday morning complete their induction by 9am?"**
Listen for the answer. If it involves admin provisioning delays or paper alternatives, workforce scalability is a problem.

**5. "What's in the audit export when an HSE inspector asks for 6 months of permits and toolbox talks?"**
Fast, clean export = good sign. Lots of manual formatting = compliance risk.

## When SafetyCulture is still the right call

Honest point: there are UK construction firms where SafetyCulture is the right fit.

- If your main use case is inspections and audits, not permits and RAMS
- If you're already on the SafetyCulture platform for other operations and want one system
- If you have a smaller workforce where per-user pricing is manageable
- If you value the mature integration ecosystem (Procore, Microsoft Teams, etc.)
- If your H&S team has capacity to build UK-specific templates in-house

In those scenarios, SafetyCulture is a strong product. The alternative conversation is mostly relevant when one or more of those conditions don't apply.

## Frequently asked questions

**How much does SafetyCulture actually cost for UK construction?**
The Premium plan is approximately $24 per user per month on annual billing. On 50 workers and 5 supervisors that's around £780/month, or £9,400/year. Enterprise pricing is custom and typically higher. Costs can increase further with add-on modules (Training, Sensors, HeadsUp).

**Does SafetyCulture have UK permit to work templates?**
The platform supports custom templates that users can build to represent UK permits. It does not come with pre-built UK permit types (hot work, confined space, etc.) with full permit lifecycle (issue, approve, pause, close-out) in the way UK-specific platforms do.

**What's the cheapest UK construction safety software?**
For small firms, HBXL Health & Safety Xpert has a low entry price but is desktop-focused. For worker-heavy sites, MySafeOps is often cheapest because worker accounts are free, removing the per-seat cost that dominates most other pricing. Exact cheapest depends on site size and feature needs.

**Is SafetyCulture good for CDM 2015 compliance?**
It can be configured for CDM compliance, but doesn't come with CDM-specific workflows pre-built. A Construction Phase Plan, Pre-Construction Information pack, or principal contractor duty checklist would all need to be built as custom templates.

**Can I switch from SafetyCulture to an alternative without losing data?**
Yes. Most platforms support data export (SafetyCulture exports as PDF, DOCX, XML, CSV). A structured migration over 4-8 weeks, running both platforms in parallel, is the usual approach. The data loss risk is low if planned properly.

**How long does it take to switch safety platforms?**
Small firms (under 30 workers) can typically switch inside 2-3 weeks. Mid-size contractors (50-200 workers) usually take 4-8 weeks including parallel running. Large firms with multiple sites can take 3-6 months for full rollout with minimal disruption.
