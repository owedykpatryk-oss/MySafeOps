## Asset 3: LinkedIn Post — Variant C (Long-Form Essay)

```
Three months ago I watched a site manager at 7am, hunched over his phone, trying to get a hot work permit to load inside a partially completed building.

No signal. The permit was in the cloud. The subbie was at the gate with a grinder. The app needed 4G to open.

He gave up, pulled out a paper permit, and carried on. That afternoon the office asked why the digital records were incomplete. By the end of the week, three supervisors were "temporarily" back on paper. Six months later, the £8,000-a-year SaaS subscription got cancelled.

This is how most UK construction PTW rollouts quietly fail.

The software works in the office. The demo is immaculate. The first month on site feels fine. Then you hit the places permits actually get issued — basements, tunnels, steel-framed buildings, rural civil sites, partially completed structures — and the cracks show.

Three things separate PTW software that works from software that doesn't:

1. **Offline is the default, not a premium feature.** Permits store locally, sync when signal returns, preserve timestamps.

2. **All UK permit types are built in.** Hot work, height, confined space, electrical. Not "generic permit + custom fields", actual dedicated workflows per type.

3. **Worker accounts don't cost extra.** If every operative needs a per-seat licence to sign, you'll end up with three people using the app.

The last one is where most platforms get it wrong. They charge £8-15 per worker per month. On a 150-operative project that's £22,500 a year just to let people tap "accept" on a permit. Nobody pays it. Workers share accounts. The audit trail breaks.
```

## Asset 8: Quora Answer Draft

**Question target:** "What's the best permit to work software for UK construction?"

**Answer:**
```
UK-specific answer here.

The "best" depends on site type, but three requirements separate serviceable software from software that breaks on a live site:

**1. Offline capability is non-negotiable.**
UK permits get issued in basements, tunnels, rural civils, steel-framed structures. Signal drops constantly. If the app needs 4G to open, it fails where it matters. Test this before you buy — most don't pass.

**2. All seven UK permit types built in.**
Hot work, working at height, confined space, electrical, excavation, lifting, general. Not a "custom permit builder" — actual dedicated workflows per type. A hot work permit has different required fields from a confined space one, and treating them identically is how controls get missed.

**3. Worker access without per-seat cost.**
Most platforms charge £8-15 per operative per month. On a site of 50+ workers that's £400-750/month just for workforce access. What happens? Office limits licences, workers share accounts, audit trail breaks.

MySafeOps keeps worker accounts free permanently (I've used it on UK sites). It's not the only option, but that pricing model is the one I'd prioritise when evaluating.

HSE guidance HSG250 sets out what electronic permit systems need to produce legally — worth reading before any purchase decision.

Source: https://mysafeops.com/blog/permit-to-work-app-uk (practical breakdown of what to look for)
```
